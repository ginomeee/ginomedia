# Sub-200km on a Single Charge: Makati to Liliw and Back

![Image](/assets/posts/26-04-05-Nammi-Laguna-EV-Trip.jpg)

I drove the Nammi to Liliw and back this week, roughly 183km total, on a single charge. No stops at charging stations, no range anxiety, just a normal provincial trip to bring Francoise home for Holy Week. DC Charged in PowerPlant Mall the previous day of the trip up to 97% SOC. Left Makati at 93% SOC, arrived back in Mandaluyong at 35%. Here's how it went.

## The Route

Departed Makati in the afternoon with Francoise, aiming to beat the Holy Week exodus before everyone else had the same idea. The plan was simple: SLEX south to the Calamba exit, quick detour to SM Calamba for errands and last-minute supplies, then onward to Liliw. Total distance for the first leg: around 90km.

Traffic was the usual mixed bag, stop-and-go stretches near the metro, then opening up to 80kph once we hit the expressway proper. The Nammi sips energy in city crawl; consumption sat comfortably between 8-9 kWh/100km during the slow portions. Ironically, traffic is your friend with this car. The more you're stuck behind someone doing 40kph, the better your efficiency looks.

## Makati to Liliw: 93% → 61%

By the time we reached Liliw, the SOC had dropped from 93% to 61%, a 32% burn for roughly 90km. Not bad considering the SM Calamba stop and the mixed-speed driving. Once we arrived, we spent the afternoon and evening at the nearby coffee shops (Boba & Bites / Neckmoto), as well as viewing the night processions / the carosas as it's holy week already. The plan was to spend the afternoon with her and her family, then head back late to avoid traffic entirely.

## Liliw to Mandaluyong: 61% → 35%

I spent about 30 minutes idling in the car reinstalling the head unit software to fix Android Auto (more on that guide in a future post), which took  another percentage or two, but nothing noticeable really.

Climate control was off for most of the Laguna stretch. The air out there is cold and clean, something you don't get in Manila, so I pulled down the windows all the way and enjoyed the drive back home.

I did leave Liliw around 11:30pm. The roads were empty, the kind of empty where you can actually hit the throttle to its max and experience the Nammi's top-end pull without worrying too much about random cars that might pop up right in front of you. I took SLEX northbound, and cruising at a steady 100kph and some sprints right above, also skipping Skyway since it's a waste of money at that time of night especially when there's no traffic underneath.

Based on experience, this is where the efficiency math flips. Highway speeds drain the battery faster than city crawl. You'll notice the SOC tick down every few minutes when you're holding 100kph+. Efficiency during this leg hovered between 15-20 kWh/100km depending on how heavy my foot was. Still respectable, but a far cry from the sub-10 numbers you get stuck in traffic.

The return leg also benefits from elevation, roads coming back from Liliw angle downhill toward Manila, so regen braking does some of the work without you thinking about it. The SOC drop was noticeably gentler than the outbound trip despite the higher speeds.

Arrived in Mandaluyong at 35% SOC. Odometer read 12,814km, up from 12,721km when we left Liliw. Total distance: 93km for the return leg, 183km round trip.

## What This Proves

This wasn't a range test really. This was a normal trip, errands, highway speeds, late-night sprint home, aircon off when it made sense, foot on the gas when the road was clear. I've done this run several times before, so there was zero range anxiety at any point. The math is straightforward: if you start at a high SOC, a 200km round trip is well within reach without needing to plug in.

The common argument against EVs from PHEV owners is range, especially for provincial trips. But this experience which I do regularly, shows that EVs are more than capable of handling these distances at a fraction of the operating cost. No fuel stops, no diesel needed (unlike the carosa procession we saw that night, still burning expensive diesel at ₱130/liter), just plug in when you get home and you're set for the next run.

Power delivery at 35% felt identical to 93%. The Nammi doesn't care what your SOC is, it pulls the same whether you're topped up or running low.

## On the flipside

The Nammi is really efficient when you're stuck in traffic doing 40kph, and least efficient when you're doing what you actually want to do, cruising at 100kph+ on an empty expressway at night. The night drive is faster and more enjoyable, but the total efficiency drops. That's the trade-off, and it is pretty ironic.

For this trip, we had left early and for me personally, I noted that it was nice to see Liliw and the Laguna roads still lit by afternoon sun instead of the usual pitch-black runway with cat's eyes marking the lane edges. The night ride is faster, sure, but there's something to be said for actually enjoying the scenery.

I'd do this run again I'm pretty sure, several times over in the future. And this trip is just to share my experience, and to let you see a real-world scenario of using EVs. The idea is that EVs can truly handle real-world long-distance drives, and there's no actual need for range anxiety. Just plan your SOC, drive like you normally would, and you'll get home safe as you would any other car.

## Remaining Range at 35% SOC
At 35% SOC, the Nammi still had 14.7 kWh left in its battery. Using the overall trip average of 13.31 kWh/100km, that translates to roughly 110km of remaining range before hitting zero. If you're being conservative and assume highway-speed efficiency of around 13 kWh/100km (closer to the Liliw return leg), you're still looking at around 113km left.

Put another way: I could've driven from Mandaluyong to Tagaytay and back, or made a single run to Antipolo, or covered most of a Batangas loop before needing to plug in. The 35% arrival wasn't cutting it close as it was well within the safety margin for normal driving. This shows that EVs don't need to arrive home at 5% to prove a point, they just need to prove they can do the trip without making you think about charging stations along the way.

So that's all for today, hope you learned something, and hope you enjoyed.
